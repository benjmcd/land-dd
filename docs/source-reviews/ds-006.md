## Provenance and caveats

- Source version/date available: ECHO data refresh schedule published on About the Data page; most databases refresh weekly; SDWIS refreshes less frequently; EIS/NEI every three years. Lag between source-database entry and ECHO display ranges from one week to three months depending on system.
- Update cadence or freshness class: weekly (most systems); up to quarterly for some systems.
- Geographic coverage limits: Regulated facilities under EPA programs (Clean Water Act, Clean Air Act, RCRA, SDWA, etc.); coverage depends on which facilities are subject to federal/delegated-state permitting. Not all environmental issues are tracked in ECHO; many hazards (historical dumps, non-permitted releases, agricultural runoff) are outside ECHO scope.
- Precision/scale limits: ECHO records facility locations; proximity of a regulated facility to a subject parcel does not establish contamination, plume extent, exposure pathway, or legal liability. Site-specific environmental due diligence (Phase I/II ESA) is required for regulatory and transactional purposes.
- Known caveats to store with evidence: (1) A nearby regulated facility does not prove subject-property contamination or legal liability. (2) ECHO reflects records in EPA program databases; unreported or non-federally-regulated releases are outside scope. (3) ECHO data may lag source entry by one week to three months. (4) EPA warrants no accuracy, completeness, or currency of ECHO data. (5) ECHO data cannot establish rights, substantive or procedural, enforceable by any party.
- Required attribution text: Credit U.S. Environmental Protection Agency / ECHO (echo.epa.gov), facility ID(s), retrieval date, and ECHO data-refresh date.
- Fields that must not be exposed: Do not use EPA/federal government seals or logos in a way that implies endorsement; do not display ECHO data as a final official EPA determination or regulatory finding.
- Source failure / no-data behavior: Persist source-failure evidence when ECHO returns an error, returns no facilities within a search area, is blocked, or is unavailable. Record the failure type, timestamp, and search parameters.
- Reviewer notes: Approved for MVP environmental compliance screening with restrictions. The critical user-reportable caveat — that a nearby regulated facility does not prove subject-property contamination — must appear in every report section sourced from ECHO. The robotic-query restriction in ECHO's terms requires that the connector use bounded, throttled queries and not initiate bulk downloads. Commercial-use rights for individual ECHO documents warrant caution; the general public-domain/informational-purpose framing covers standard API screening use.
