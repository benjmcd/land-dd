## Provenance and caveats

- Source version/date available: Metadata published date, project metadata, imagery/source date, and NWI download update cycle where available.
- Update cadence or freshness class: biannual; NWI updates are reflected in Wetlands Mapper and downloads in October and May.
- Geographic coverage limits: National coverage varies by mapping status, project metadata availability, and digital mapping completion; some locations may lack project or historic metadata.
- Precision/scale limits: NWI mapping depends heavily on aerial imagery and project-level methods; exclusions and omissions are documented for some habitat types.
- Known caveats to store with evidence: Wetland/deepwater screening only; not a jurisdictional delineation, Clean Water Act determination, permit decision, legal conclusion, buildability conclusion, or substitute for field delineation/professional review.
- Required attribution text: U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, National Wetlands Inventory, source URL, access date, metadata published date, and project metadata where available.
- Fields that must not be exposed: Do not use FWS logos or marks in a way that implies endorsement; preserve any marked non-public-domain material notices.
- Source failure / no-data behavior: Persist source-failure evidence when NWI data are unavailable for the area, mapping status is incomplete, metadata is missing, downloads/services fail, or query bounds are too large.
- Reviewer notes: Approved for MVP wetland/deepwater screening use with restrictions; connector implementation still must define bounded geography, metadata capture, refresh handling, and report caveat mapping.
