## Provenance and caveats

- Source version/date available: Instantaneous values typically at 15-minute intervals transmitted hourly; daily values computed from instantaneous; site metadata versioned by USGS. Provisional data may be revised.
- Update cadence or freshness class: dynamic; real-time data typically delayed by less than one hour; daily values lag by one day; approved (quality-assured) data may lag by months.
- Geographic coverage limits: USGS monitoring network; coverage varies by watershed, state, and parameter. Not all sites measure all parameters. Many rural parcels have no proximate monitoring station.
- Precision/scale limits: Monitoring stations are fixed points; streamflow/groundwater readings reflect conditions at the site, not at a subject parcel. Proximity of a monitoring station to a parcel is a weak proxy at best. USGS does not represent that monitoring data establishes water availability, well viability, or water rights for any parcel.
- Known caveats to store with evidence: (1) Monitoring proximity may be a weak proxy for actual water availability at the subject parcel. (2) Provisional data are subject to revision and should not be used for decisions with substantial monetary or safety consequences. (3) Monitoring data does not establish water rights, legal water access, well viability, or supply adequacy. (4) Absence of a monitoring station near a parcel does not indicate absence of water resources.
- Required attribution text: Credit U.S. Geological Survey / Water Data for the Nation (waterdata.usgs.gov), service URL, site code(s), parameter code(s), retrieval date, and provisional/approved status.
- Fields that must not be exposed: Do not use USGS trademarked identifiers or official marks in a way that implies endorsement or official determination.
- Source failure / no-data behavior: Persist source-failure evidence when USGS water services return an error, return no sites within a search area, return no data for a requested parameter/date range, or are blocked (HTTP 403). Record the failure type and timestamp.
- Reviewer notes: Approved for MVP water monitoring context with restrictions. Provisional data handling is critical: the connector must stamp provisional status on every record and surface it in reports. The most important user-reportable caveat is that monitoring proximity may be a weak proxy for actual water availability, and no water-rights or well-viability conclusion may be drawn from this source.
