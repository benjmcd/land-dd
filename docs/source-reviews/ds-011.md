## Assessed-value caveat — mandatory

**Assessed values produced by county assessors are tax-administration artifacts.** In North Carolina, assessed value is set as of January 1 of the reappraisal year under NC G.S. § 105-283. It is a point-in-time estimate used to allocate the tax burden among property owners in a given county tax cycle.

**Assessed value is NOT:**
- An independent market appraisal
- A lender appraisal or lending-suitability indicator
- A real estate broker price opinion or list-price estimate
- A measure of investment return, resale price, or liquidation value
- A current estimate of value between appraisal cycles

Counties are required to reappraise on at most an eight-year cycle; mid-cycle values may lag market conditions materially. Buncombe delayed its 2025 reappraisal to 2026 due to Tropical Storm Helene; affected parcels may still be in process. Chatham and Brunswick values reflect their respective reappraisal dates.

No report, display, API response, or export derived from DS-011 may assert, imply, or suggest that an assessed value represents market value, appraisal value, investment value, lending collateral value, or any other non-tax valuation conclusion. Every use of assessed value data must carry the caveat: "Assessed value is a tax-administration figure effective as of the county's last reappraisal date and is not a market appraisal, lender appraisal, or investment-suitability indicator."

## Legal framework: NC public records

Property tax and assessor records are public records under NC G.S. § 132-1. The statute establishes that "public records and public information compiled by the agencies of North Carolina government or its subdivisions are the property of the people." Citizens may obtain copies "free or at minimal cost." No political subdivision may enter into a nondisclosure agreement to restrict access to public records subject to disclosure under Chapter 132.

NC G.S. § 105-283 requires that property be appraised at "true value in money," which the statute defines as equivalent to market value for tax-appraisal purposes. This is the statutory basis for county assessor valuations but does not make assessed values equivalent to independent market appraisals, lender appraisals, or investment valuations.

## Provenance and caveats

- Update cadence or freshness class: periodic; NC law allows up to an 8-year reappraisal cycle; individual parcel updates may occur between cycles for new construction, demolition, splits, or corrections.
- Geographic coverage limits: Three counties only (Buncombe, Chatham, Brunswick NC — private-MVP scope). All other counties are NOT_EVALUATED.
- Precision/scale limits: Assessed value is a tax-administrative estimate as of the reappraisal date; it is not a real-time or continuous market estimate. Mid-cycle values may be materially stale. Buncombe 2025 reappraisal was delayed to 2026 due to Tropical Storm Helene; some affected parcels may remain in process.
- Known caveats to store with evidence: (1) Assessed value is a tax-administration artifact effective as of the reappraisal date; it is not a market appraisal, lender appraisal, investment-value estimate, or lending-suitability indicator. (2) County disclaimers state no warranty of accuracy or completeness. (3) Owner/value/sale-history fields are suppressed. (4) No programmatic access terms reviewed for any county; machine access requires county confirmation.
- Required attribution text: "[County name] Tax Administration / Assessor" with source portal URL, retrieval date, and reappraisal effective date.
- Fields that must not be exposed: Owner name, owner address, sale history, delinquent tax status, exemption detail — not to be exposed in any report, display, API response, or export.
- Source failure / no-data behavior: When assessor data is unavailable or not evaluated for an AOI, emit a NOT_EVALUATED sentinel (ASSESSOR_NOT_EVALUATED) as first-class evidence. Do not substitute silence or a no-issue-found result.
