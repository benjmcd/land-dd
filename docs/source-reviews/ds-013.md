## Provenance and caveats

- Source version/date available: Unknown — NC DEQ well registry records are submitted by licensed well drillers; record completeness varies; older records may lack GPS coordinates.
- Update cadence or freshness class: Semi-static; new well construction permits submitted as wells are drilled; records may be updated for corrections; completeness of spatial data depends on driller submission.
- Geographic coverage limits: NC statewide but coverage is non-uniform; older wells may lack spatial coordinates; rural areas may have unregistered wells (dug/driven wells pre-regulation). Chatham, Buncombe, and Brunswick counties are within NC scope.
- Precision/scale limits: Location data is permit-based and may represent approximate parcel centroid rather than exact well head. Depth and aquifer fields are driller-reported and not independently verified. Proximity of registered wells to a subject parcel does not confirm actual on-parcel water availability.
- Known caveats to store with evidence: (1) Registered wells are a proxy for groundwater potential, not a water-availability guarantee. (2) Older or undocumented wells may not appear. (3) Registry coordinates may be approximate. (4) Well log data does not establish legal water rights, yield adequacy, or well viability for the subject parcel. (5) Well construction records are public but may contain driller-identifying information; do not expose as ownership data.
- Required attribution text: NC Division of Water Resources, NC Department of Environmental Quality (NC DEQ); retrieval date; registry version/date if available.
- Fields that must not be exposed: Owner name if present; driller name/license as PII proxy if not appropriate to display; direct parcel ownership assertions.
- Source failure / no-data behavior: Persist source-failure evidence when registry API is unavailable, returns authentication error, or returns no records within search area.
- Reviewer notes: **PRIMARY BLOCKER: No public unauthenticated REST API confirmed.** The NC OneMap ArcGIS service for the well registry requires a registered NC OneMap token. No public download API endpoint was accessible via automated research. Static downloads from NC OneMap may be available via the website but require manual browser interaction. A live connector cannot be implemented without either: (a) a registered NC OneMap token with confirmed license for automated use, or (b) identification of an alternative public API endpoint (e.g., a static GeoJSON or CSV download with a publicly accessible URL). NC DEQ website structure has changed significantly from prior documentation. Re-review required when a public endpoint or confirmed static download is identified.
